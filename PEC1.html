VERBAL VIOLENCE
Verbal abuse (also verbal assault or aggression) occurs when one person criticizes or insults another person. It is a destructive form of communication intended to damage the other person's self-concept and produce negative emotions. Verbal abuse is a maladaptive defense mechanism that anyone may engage in occasionally, such as during times of great stress or physical discomfort. For some people, it is a pattern of behaviors used intentionally to control or manipulate others or to exact revenge.
PHYSICAL VIOLENCE
It is the use of physical force to cause non-accidental harm; or with some type of weapon or object that may or may not cause injuries, whether internal, external, or both. This type of violence is more easily recognized; it leaves injuries on the body that may be permanent, cause some disability or even cause death. The fear that the victim experiences at the possibility of the violent episode being repeated, can keep them in a state of tension that generates physical discomforts such as headaches, gastrointestinal disorders, tachycardia, etc.
